Farai Mudzingwa is a writer whose fiction, in his own words, is influenced by music and alternate realities. His debut novel *Avenues by Train* (Cassava Republic Press, 2023), shortlisted for the Republic of Consciousness Prize, navigates post-independence Zimbabwe through ancestral spirits, childhood trauma, and the sidelined epistemologies of a colonised people. His novella *Green Shadows in the Kiya Kiya Republic* moves through a dystopian, mythical post-coup Zimbabwe where corruption is atmospheric, almost ecological. Both works revolt, love and dance through what it costs to navigate systems, political, spiritual, and historical, that were never designed to accommodate him.

The two digital works in this portfolio extend that thematic focus into new media. A maker-scholar practice rooted in the intimate and the political, in grief held privately and injustice experienced publicly. Both the Bitsy game and the Twine game embrace game studies and propose that a work's structure is its argument. That how readers/players are asked to move through something is inseparable from what it is trying to say. In these works, the scholarship is the making.
